Crisis support

If you're thinking about ending it

First: the fact that you clicked this page is not weakness. It's the part of you that wants to live doing its job. Listen to that part for the next two minutes.

Suicidal thinking is a symptom, not a verdict. It shows up when pain outlasts your sense of options — and both of those things change, even when your brain is insisting they won't. The feeling that it will always be like this is the single most reliable lie this state tells. You don't have to believe us. You just have to stay, and let someone help you carry it while the state passes.

Talk to someone now — free, anonymous, 24/7

Call or text 988 (US)

The Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. A trained human, not a script — they listen first, and you can hang up any time. Most calls are just a conversation; emergency services get involved only in rare, imminent-danger situations. Call 988, or chat online if voice feels like too much. Veterans: press 1 after dialing.

Text HOME to 741741

Crisis Text Line, if talking out loud feels impossible right now. The whole conversation happens over text.

LGBTQ+ and under 25

The Trevor Project: call 1-866-488-7386, text START to 678678, or chat online — counselors who get the specific weight you're carrying.

Outside the US

findahelpline.com lists verified crisis lines for nearly every country, filtered by what you're going through.

If you've already done something, or you're about to

Call 911 (or your local emergency number) or get to an emergency room. This outranks everything else on this page.

While you wait, or if you can't call yet

  • Put time and distance between you and the means. Give the pills to someone, leave the location, get out of the room. Suicidal intensity comes in waves — most acute crises pass in minutes to hours, and distance from the means is the single most protective move there is.
  • Tell one person something true. "I'm not okay and I'm scared of what I'm thinking" is a complete sentence. You don't owe anyone a calm, well-organized version.
  • Don't decide tonight. You don't have to feel hopeful. You just have to defer the question while your nervous system is flooded. Sleep changes brain states. Let it.

If it's not tonight, but it's been dark for a while

Passive thoughts — "everyone would be better off," "I just don't want to exist" — deserve real care too, before they sharpen. That means a therapist or a doctor, not just willpower and a website. The full list of care resources is here — counseling directories, sliding-scale options, addiction help, all of it.

One more thing. The verdict "burden" is the state talking, not the facts. The people who love you would rather carry this with you for years than live without you for one. Smoldering still counts as lit. Stay.
