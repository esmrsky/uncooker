The pitch, straight up

The stuff keeping you stuck isn't mysterious anymore. Dopamine tolerance, social-rank circuitry, loneliness hypervigilance, rumination loops, threat-scanning run amok — the mechanics of modern misery are mapped, named, and published. And the practices that reverse them are too: deliberate abstinence, gratitude, structured forgiveness, committed community, service, rest, retrained attention. Real studies, real effect sizes, real researchers — cited on every page.

So that's what this site is: six loops, the mechanism behind each, and a concrete protocol out — specific enough to start this week, honest about what a website can and can't do for you.

One more thing, for the curious: these counter-practices weren't invented by the studies that validated them. They show up, assembled into a single coherent way of life, in texts that are two thousand years old. Each pattern page has a small optional card — the old map — showing the overlap, and the full version of that argument lives here. Open it if it interests you. Skip it if it doesn't. The science and the protocols stand on their own.

The six ways people get cooked

Every rut we've ever seen runs on one or more of these. Each page covers how the loop works, what it's doing to your brain, and the counter-practice — concrete enough to start this week.

What this site is not

Not therapy, and not a substitute for it — and definitely not a substitute for a doctor if you're in real trouble. Medication is not a personal failure, and a counselor is not a backup plan — for a lot of people they're the front line, and the practices here work alongside them, not instead of them. If you need actual help right now, it's here.
